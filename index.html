Section 01

What is Panchayati Raj?

Local Government

A system of local self-government for rural areas in India

People's Power

Village people make decisions for their own development

Democratic Roots

Modern system began in 1959; constitutionally mandated by the 73rd Amendment Act, 1992 (enforced on April 24, 1993)

Section 02

Why is it Important?

Decentralization

Power moves from state to local villages

People Participation

Villagers directly take part in decisions

Rural Development

Better roads, schools, and healthcare

Social Justice

SC, ST, and women get reserved seats (at least one‑third for women)

Local Solutions

Local problems get local solutions

Self Reliance

Villages learn to manage themselves

Section 03

The 3-Tier System

Three levels of rural local government

Zila Parishad

District Level

Panchayat Samiti

Block Level

Gram Panchayat

Village Level

Note: In states with population below 20 lakh, the middle tier may be optional, but the three‑tier system is the standard constitutional framework.

Section 04

Gram Panchayat

Village Level Government

Roads & Transport

Build and maintain village roads

Water Supply

Clean drinking water for all

Primary Education

Manage and oversee village schools (implementation often coordinated with higher tiers)

Health & Sanitation

Healthcare and cleanliness

Agriculture

Support farmers and farming

Record Keeping

Birth, death, and village census records (land records maintained by the revenue department)

Section 05

Samiti & Parishad

Panchayat Samiti

Block Level • Intermediate Level

  • ✓ Works at Block (Tehsil) level
  • ✓ Coordinates Gram Panchayats
  • ✓ Implements government schemes
  • ✓ Manages block development funds
  • ✓ Plans for area development

Zila Parishad

District Level • Top Level

  • ✓ Works at District level
  • ✓ Coordinates Panchayat Samitis
  • ✓ Distributes funds to blocks
  • ✓ District planning and budget
  • ✓ Supervises all rural works

Section 06

The 73rd Amendment

1992

Constitutional Amendment Act

1
Constitutional Status

Gave Panchayats legal recognition (Part IX of the Constitution)

2
Regular Elections

Elections every 5 years mandatory; if dissolved, fresh elections within 6 months

3
Reservation

Seats for SC, ST, and not less than one‑third for women

4
3-Tier System

Made three‑level system compulsory (with exception for small states)

5
State Election Commission

Independent body for fair elections

6
Financial Powers

State govts. authorise Panchayats to collect specific taxes as recommended by the State Finance Commission
